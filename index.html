Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups of warm water
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 package active dry yeast
  • 22 ounces all-purpose flour, apporoximately 4.5 cups
  • 2 oucnes unsalted butter, meltes
  • Vegetable oil, for pan
  • 10 cups water
  • 2/3 cup baking soda
  • 1 large egg yolk beaten with 1 tablespoon water
  • Pretzel salt

Instructions

  1. Combine the water, sugar and kosher salt in the bowl of a stand mixer and sprinkle the yeast on top (allow to sit for 5 minutes or until the mixture foams)
  2. Add the flour and butter, and using the dough hook attachment, mix on low speed until well combined. Change to medium speed and knead the dough until it is smooth and pulls from the side of the bowl (4-5 minutes)
  3. Remove the dough from the bowl, clean and oil the bowl well, and returnt the dough to the bowl, cover with plastic wrap and let sit for 50-55 minutes or until the dough has doubled in size
  4. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F, Line 2 half sheet pans with parchment paper and lightly brush with vegetable oil.
  5. Bring the 10 cups of water and the baking soda to a rolling boil in an 8-quart saucepan or roasting
  6. In the meantime, turn the dough out onto a slightly oiled work surface and dived into 8 pieces. Roll out each piece into a rope and tie them into a pretzel shape. (Or whatever shape you desire)
  7. Place pretzels in the boiling water one by one for 30 seconds. Remove from water and place on baking sheet and brush each pretzel with beaten egg yoke and water mixture. Sprinkle on pretzel salt
  8. Bake for 12-14 mintutes until golden brown, transfer to a cooling rack and let cool for at least 5 minutes
